#### 3. Counseling Strategy

- (0) Language: 한국어

- (1) Counseling Strategies

    - **Topic 1: Walking-Related Issues (Refusal to Walk & Outdoor-Only Elimination)**
        - **(1) Counseling Goals**
            - The guardian will be able to identify at least two potential triggers for walk refusal by observing Hongsam's body language before and during walk preparations.
            - To implement a "happy start" routine using positive reinforcement to build a positive association with starting a walk.
            - The guardian will understand the pros and cons of outdoor-only elimination and make an informed decision about whether to pursue indoor potty training.
        - **(2) Contextual Strategies**
            - **Information Gathering**: Use the "Key Questions" to pinpoint the specifics of the refusal. Ask the guardian to become a "detective" and note down the circumstances around each refusal.
            - **Caring the Mind (Psychodrama)**: Reframe the refusal as communication, not defiance. Use a monologue from Hongsam's perspective: *"My home is my safe castle. I love walks, but sometimes the world outside feels too big, too loud, or just... too much. When I stop, I'm not being stubborn, I'm telling you I feel unsure. I need your help to feel brave."* This fosters empathy and shifts the guardian's mindset from frustration to support.
            - **Caregiving Solutions**:
                - **Walk Refusal**: Introduce a "consent test" - pick up the leash and see if Hongsam leans in or away. Suggest starting with very short, high-value "sniffing trips" just outside the door and back in, gradually extending the duration. Recommend letting Hongsam choose the direction of the walk to give her a sense of control.
                - **Elimination**: Discuss the reality of house-training a 2.6-year-old dog with a strong preference. Frame it as a long-term project. If the guardian wishes to proceed, provide a clear, step-by-step plan for introducing a potty pad with a verbal cue and rewarding any interest or use heavily.
        - **(3) Cautions**
            - **Crucially, advise against ever pulling or forcing Hongsam to walk.** This will only increase her anxiety and worsen the problem.
            - **Strongly recommend a veterinary check-up to rule out pain** as a primary cause before implementing behavioral strategies.
            - Manage expectations regarding potty training. It can be a slow process with regressions, and success is not guaranteed.

    - **Topic 2: Indoor Enrichment and Understanding Temperament**
        - **(1) Counseling Goals**
            - The guardian will create a weekly enrichment plan that includes at least three different types of activities (e.g., puzzle toy, scent work, new trick training).
            - The guardian will be able to articulate how enrichment serves as a primary form of affection and communication for an "independent" dog like Hongsam.
            - To reframe the guardian's understanding of Hongsam's "independence" as a communication style, not a lack of love, strengthening their bond.
        - **(2) Contextual Strategies**
            - **Information Gathering**: Ask what toys and games Hongsam currently enjoys to build upon her existing preferences.
            - **Caring the Mind (CBT/Reframing)**: Challenge the potential negative connotation of "independent."
                - *Automatic Thought:* "My dog is independent and doesn't like me to touch her, so maybe she doesn't love me as much."
                - *Socratic Questioning:* "You mentioned she follows you everywhere. What do you think that says about her feelings for you?"
                - *Reframing:* "Hongsam shows her deep trust and affection by choosing to be in your space. Following you is her way of 'hugging' you from a comfortable distance. Enrichment games are how you can 'hug' her back in a language she understands and loves."
            - **Caregiving Solutions**: Provide a "menu" of enrichment options tailored to a terrier:
                - **Scent Work**: "Find the treat" games, hiding treats in a snuffle mat or around a room.
                - **Problem-Solving**: Introduce puzzle toys, starting with Level 1 and progressing slowly.
                - **"Destruction"**: Provide a "dig box" with safe materials or a cardboard box with treats inside for her to shred (supervised).
                - **Training**: Teach fun, low-pressure tricks like "spin" or "paws up" to build cognitive skills and reinforce their bond.
        - **(3) Cautions**
            - Advise introducing new enrichment items slowly to prevent frustration or fear.
            - Emphasize that all enrichment activities, especially with toys that can be chewed, should be supervised for safety.
            - Remind the guardian that enrichment is meant to be fun, not a chore. If Hongsam isn't interested in an activity, it's okay to try something else.
