#### 3. Counseling Strategy

- (0) Language: 한국어

- (1) Counseling Strategies

    - **Topic 1: Fear-based Reactivity Towards the Father**
        - **(1) Counseling Goals**
            - To help the guardian reframe Dodo's barking and growling from "bad behavior" to "communication of fear," fostering empathy.
            - To educate the guardian on the critical role of the socialization period and how its absence has shaped Dodo's current worldview.
            - To provide the guardian with simple, safe management techniques to immediately lower Dodo's daily stress levels and prevent the father from triggering her.
            - To empower the guardian to become a "behavior detective" to identify the specific micro-triggers associated with her father's actions.

        - **(2) Contextual Strategies**
            - **Phase 1: Validate and Educate.** Start by validating the guardian's love for Dodo and praising her for seeking to understand the 'why'. "It's wonderful that you're not just asking how to stop the barking, but why it's happening. That shows how deeply you care for Dodo's well-being." Then, gently introduce the concept of the socialization period. Use an analogy: "Imagine a child who only ever met one type of person. When they finally meet someone very different, it can be scary. For Dodo, who met very few people as a puppy, your father might seem like a complete unknown, and that's frightening for her."
            - **Phase 2: Reframe using Psychodrama.** Use a monologue to voice Dodo's perspective. "If Dodo could talk, she might say, 'My person is my safe place. But that other big person in the house... his movements are unpredictable and his voice is loud. I don't know what he wants. I have to bark to keep him away and protect myself and my safe person. I'm not being bad, I'm just really scared.'" This shifts the focus from disobedience to emotion.
            - **Phase 3: Introduce Management Solutions (not training).** Since the guardian wants analysis first, focus on immediate management to reduce stress. Suggest creating a "Dodo's Safe Haven" – a comfy bed or open crate in a corner of the room where the father agrees never to approach. This gives Dodo a place to retreat to. Also, suggest the father could try tossing a high-value treat in Dodo's direction *without looking at her or talking to her* whenever he enters a room or gets up. This begins to build a new, positive association (Classical Counter-Conditioning).
            - **Phase 4: Empower the Guardian with CBT.** Encourage the guardian to challenge the automatic thought "Dodo hates my dad." Ask her to gather evidence. "For the next week, could you be a detective? Let's make a small log. Note exactly what your father was doing when Dodo barked. Was he standing up? Walking toward you? Reaching for something? This will give us the clues we need to truly understand Dodo's triggers."

        - **(3) Cautions**
            - **Do NOT blame the father or create family conflict.** Emphasize that this is about Dodo's perception, not anyone's fault. The solution requires teamwork, not blame.
            - **Strongly advise against any form of punishment.** Clearly explain that punishing a growl or bark will suppress the warning signal, but not the underlying fear. This can make a dog more likely to bite without warning in the future. The growl is vital information.
            - **Manage expectations.** A fear that has been ingrained for 7 years will not disappear quickly. The goal is management and gradual improvement of Dodo's emotional state, not a quick fix.
            - **Address the "no aggression" misconception.** Gently explain that from a dog behavior perspective, growling, barking, and lunging are forms of aggressive communication used to create distance. Acknowledge that the guardian knows Dodo is not malicious, but that these signals must be taken seriously to ensure everyone's safety.
            - **Recommend a veterinary check-up.** To be thorough, recommend a vet visit to rule out any underlying pain or medical conditions (e.g., arthritis, vision/hearing issues) that could be lowering Dodo's tolerance for stress.
